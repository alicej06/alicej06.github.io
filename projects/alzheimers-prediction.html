Overview

As an undergraduate research assistant at the USC Irimia Neuroimaging Lab, I design and implement machine learning pipelines that integrate neuroimaging data (MRI, fMRI), cognitive assessments, and clinical records to predict conversion to mild cognitive impairment (MCI) and Alzheimer's disease.

This work is part of a broader research effort to enable earlier, non-invasive detection of neurodegeneration — currently in the paper-writing stage.

Technical Approach

  • Preprocessing of structural and functional MRI data: skull stripping, registration, parcellation
  • Feature extraction from brain connectivity matrices (functional connectomes) and morphometric features
  • Multi-modal fusion of neuroimaging features with cognitive test scores (MMSE, MoCA) and clinical biomarkers
  • Comparative evaluation of classical ML (SVM, Random Forest) and deep learning (CNN, GNN) classifiers
  • Longitudinal analysis to track patient trajectories and identify early conversion signatures

Current Status

  • Model development and validation ongoing at USC Irimia Neuroimaging Lab
  • Writing research paper documenting methodology and findings
  • USC CURVE Fellow — awarded $1,250 to support independent undergraduate research
