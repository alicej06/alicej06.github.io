Overview

Built as part of USC's Center of AI in Society Student Branch (CAIS++), this computer vision pipeline automates large-scale structural damage assessment by analyzing paired pre- and post-disaster satellite imagery.

The system is designed to accelerate disaster response by replacing manual inspection — a slow, costly, and dangerous process — with automated change detection at scale.

Technical Approach

  • Siamese CNN architecture trained on paired pre/post-disaster satellite image crops to detect structural damage
  • Semantic segmentation for building footprint extraction from high-resolution aerial imagery
  • Change detection module combining difference maps with attention mechanisms for fine-grained damage classification
  • Trained and evaluated on XVIEW2 disaster dataset; achieved 96% classification accuracy
  • Full PyTorch pipeline with data augmentation for varying satellite angles, resolutions, and seasonal differences

Applications

  • Rapid triage of disaster zones to prioritize emergency response teams
  • Scalable assessment across large geographic areas within hours of event
  • Applicable to earthquakes, floods, hurricanes, and wildfire structural damage
