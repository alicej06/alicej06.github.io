ShadeLA
Geospatial ML for equitable shade deployment ahead of LA2028 Olympics
Geospatial ML Optimization Python GIS Urban Planning CAIS++ Social Impact

Overview

ShadeLA is a geospatial optimization project developed through USC's Center of AI in Society Student Branch (CAIS++). It builds ML models to prioritize equitable shade deployment across Los Angeles ahead of the 2028 Olympics, with findings presented to city stakeholders to inform infrastructure planning.

The project addresses a real equity gap: low-income neighborhoods in LA consistently have less tree canopy and shade coverage, exposing residents to greater heat risk — a problem that intensifies during large-scale events like the Olympics.

Technical Approach

  • Built geospatial ML models combining satellite imagery, tree canopy data, demographics, and pedestrian traffic
  • Optimization framework to prioritize shade installation locations by impact per dollar, weighting equity metrics
  • Feature engineering from GIS layers (Census data, LIDAR tree canopy, heat island maps, event venue proximity)
  • Visualization dashboards for city planners to explore tradeoffs between equity, cost, and logistics

Impact

  • Findings presented directly to Los Angeles city stakeholders to inform LA2028 infrastructure planning
  • Research oriented toward demonstrable equity outcomes for underserved communities
  • Part of CAIS++ applied AI for social good research track
